Tribals, Dikus & the Vision of a Golden Age — quick one-word terms & definitions

Download PDF Print
TermOne-Line Definition
TribalIndigenous community living in forests/hills with distinct culture.
JhumShifting cultivation where land is cleared, burnt, and used briefly.
BehoFestival of Bodos (Assam) celebrating harvest and community life.
DikusOutsiders like moneylenders, traders, zamindars, officials who exploited tribals.
MahuaForest tree whose flowers/fruits are used for food and drink.
SalHardwood tree; tribals used its wood for houses/tools.
Birsa MundaTribal leader of the Munda Rebellion (Ulgulan) in Chotanagpur.
Ulgulan“Great Tumult” – Munda revolt (1899-1900) against British & dikus.
KhilafatSacred land in Munda belief where spirits dwell.
Forest ActBritish law restricting forest use and shifting cultivation.
ReservationArea set aside by British for timber exploitation.
PlantationLarge estate for crops like tea, coffee, rubber for export.
HeadmanVillage elder who collected rent/taxes before British interference.
Settled CultivationPermanent farming on the same plot yearly.
HulSanthal rebellion (1855–56) against zamindars, moneylenders, British.
Spirit WorshipTribal belief in ancestral/nature spirits for protection.
Golden AgeBirsa’s dream of a free tribal land without British or dikus.

Quick Points

  • Impact of British Rule: Loss of forest rights, forced labour, high taxes, exploitation by moneylenders.
  • Tribal Movements: Santhal Hul (1855), Munda Ulgulan (1899), various forest protests.
  • Birsa’s Vision: Return to ancestral customs, end of missionary influence, land free of outsiders.
  • British Strategy: Forest Acts, reserved forests, plantation economy to earn revenue.
